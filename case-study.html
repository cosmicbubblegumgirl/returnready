Product design case study

Designing reverse logistics around clarity, revenue retention, and intake readiness.

ReturnReady was framed as a two-sided product: shoppers need confidence and support teams need fewer avoidable tickets, while warehouse teams need better parcel context before intake.

Problem

Returns were expensive because customers, support, and warehouse teams saw different versions of the truth.

UX strategy

Move eligibility, evidence, and exchange fit upstream before support or warehouse work begins.

Outcome

A polished product site with 100 demo records, login, local database actions, analytics, and backend routes.

Design principles

Every screen answers the same question: what should happen next?

01Show return eligibility before the customer writes a support message.
02Recommend exchanges when inventory and condition make them viable.
03Collect evidence in a way that prepares warehouse inspection.
04Turn every return into a route, label, owner, and status.
